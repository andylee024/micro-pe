### Research Methodology

Execute these 5 phases sequentially:

#### PHASE 1: Market Intelligence (2-3 hours)
**Use WebSearch extensively to gather:**

1. **Market Size & Growth**
   - Search: "[business type] market size 2026", "[business type] industry revenue"
   - Find: US TAM, historical growth (5-year CAGR), projected growth (2026-2031)
   - Quantify: $X-Y billion TAM, X% CAGR
   - Sources: IBISWorld, Statista, trade associations, market research firms

2. **Regulatory Environment**
   - Search: "[business type] licensing requirements California", "[business type] certification"
   - Find: Specific license names, issuing bodies (state boards, professional associations)
   - Document: Renewal requirements, fees, continuing education
   - Critical: Determine if licenses are transferable with business sale

3. **Competitive Landscape**
   - Search: "[business type] companies", "top [business type] providers", "[business type] market share"
   - Find: Total number of operators, market fragmentation, major players
   - Quantify: X,XXX total businesses, top 4 market share X%, SMB % of market
   - Sources: Industry directories, trade associations, business databases

4. **Financial Benchmarks**
   - Search: "[business type] profit margins", "[business type] EBITDA margins", "[business type] revenue per employee"
   - Find: Gross margins, EBITDA margins, revenue per employee, typical multiples
   - Quantify: X-Y% gross margin, X-Y% EBITDA, $X-Y revenue/employee
   - Sources: Industry reports, RMA, Vertical IQ, financial publications

5. **M&A Activity**
   - Search: "[business type] acquisition", "[business type] private equity", "[business type] sold"
   - Find: Recent transactions (last 12-24 months), active buyers, valuation multiples
   - Document: Deal values, strategic buyers, PE firms active in space
   - Sources: M&A databases, business news, broker announcements

#### PHASE 2: Deal Flow Validation (1-2 hours)
**Critical: Validate actual businesses are for sale**

1. **BizBuySell Search**
   - Go to bizbuysell.com
   - Search relevant keywords (provide 3-5 search terms based on business type)
   - Document: Number of listings, asking prices, revenue/SDE figures, geography
   - Calculate: Implied valuation multiples (Asking Price / SDE)
   - Screenshot or list specific examples

2. **Geographic Analysis**
   - How many listings in Southern California?
   - What metros have concentration? (LA, OC, SD, IE)
   - Is deal flow sufficient? (Need 10+ targets minimum)

3. **Listing Quality Assessment**
   - Average time on market
   - Asking price reasonableness (vs. market multiples)
   - Seller motivation signals (retirement, relocation, burnout)
   - Business quality indicators (revenue growth, employee count, years in business)

#### PHASE 3: Operational Understanding (1-2 hours)
**Understand day-to-day business operations**

1. **Service Delivery Model**
   - Search: "[business type] day in the life", "[business type] process", "[business type] how it works"
   - Understand: Field work vs. office work ratio, typical job duration, customer interaction model
   - Find: YouTube videos of operators, blog posts, trade publication "day in life" articles
   - Assess: Is this remote-manageable or requires daily on-site presence?

2. **Staffing & Labor**
   - Search: "[business type] technician salary California", "[business type] jobs", "[business type] hiring"
   - Understand: Typical org structure (owner + X employees), required skills/certifications
   - Find: Salary ranges (Indeed, Glassdoor), labor availability, turnover rates
   - Assess: Can we hire/retain employees or is labor market too tight?

3. **Equipment & Technology**
   - Search: "[business type] equipment", "[business type] software", "[business type] technology"
   - Understand: Required capital equipment (costs, useful life), software systems used
   - Find: Equipment vendors, software providers (G2, Capterra reviews)
   - Assess: Automation opportunities, technology gaps creating competitive advantage

#### PHASE 4: Risk Assessment (1 hour)
**Identify deal-breakers and major risks**

1. **AI Disruption Analysis**
   - Break down work into components: What % physical? What % judgment-based? What % routine?
   - Search: "[business type] automation", "[business type] AI", "[business type] robotics", "[business type] IoT"
   - Assess: Can AI/robotics replace core work in 3-5 years? (Score: X/10 disruption risk)
   - Identify: Which tasks are AI-proof vs. automatable (physical presence = good)

2. **Macro Exposure**
   - Search: "[business type] industry trends 2026", "[business type] forecast", "[business type] challenges"
   - Identify: Growth drivers (tailwinds) and risk factors (headwinds)
   - Quantify: Impact of reshoring, regulatory changes, technology shifts
   - Assess: Net positive or negative macro environment?

3. **Regulatory Risk**
   - Search: "[business type] regulations changing", "[business type] deregulation risk", "[business type] compliance trends"
   - Assess: Are mandates stable or at risk of repeal/loosening?
   - Identify: Proposed regulatory changes (positive or negative for demand)
   - Quantify: Impact on recurring revenue if regulations change

#### PHASE 5: Financial Modeling (1-2 hours)
**Build acquisition model to calculate expected returns**

1. **Gather Inputs from BizBuySell**
   - Use 3-5 actual listings to establish baseline
   - Document: Revenue, SDE/EBITDA, asking price, number of employees, recurring revenue %
   - Calculate: Average entry multiple (X.Xx SDE)

2. **Build Base Case Model**
   - Acquisition: Purchase price, working capital, closing costs
   - Pro Forma Year 1-5: Revenue (growth assumptions), COGS, Operating Expenses, EBITDA
   - Adjustments: Remove owner salary, add manager cost, technology investments
   - Exit: Year 5 EBITDA x exit multiple (research strategic buyer/PE multiples)
   - Calculate: IRR, MOIC, cumulative cash distributions

3. **Scenario Analysis**
   - Bull Case: +20% revenue growth, margin expansion, higher exit multiple
   - Base Case: Steady growth, moderate improvements
   - Bear Case: -20% revenue decline, margin compression, lower exit multiple
   - Sensitivity: Test impact of key variables (revenue, margins, exit multiple, entry price)

4. **Return Hurdle Check**
   - Does Base Case hit 20%+ IRR? (Required minimum)
   - Is Bear Case survivable? (>0% IRR or small loss acceptable)
   - Is Bull Case realistic? (Can we execute value creation plan?)

## Report Structure (10 Sections)

Produce a comprehensive markdown report with these exact sections:

### 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
- **Investment Recommendation:** STRONG BUY / BUY / HOLD / AVOID
- **Conviction Level:** X/10 with rationale
- **Capital Allocation Priority:** Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3
- **One-Paragraph Overview:** What is this business type, why does it exist, who are customers

- **Financial Snapshot:** Table with TAM, SAM, margins, multiples, IRR
- **Go/No-Go Criteria:** Clear decision framework

### 2. BUSINESS MODEL & OPERATIONS
- **What This Business Does:** Detailed 2-3 paragraph explanation
- **Customer Profile:** Primary/secondary customers, size, geography
- **Service Delivery Model:** Field vs. office work, frequency, duration, travel
- **Revenue Model:** Revenue streams breakdown, pricing structure
- **Recurring Revenue Analysis:** Mandated vs. contractual vs. transactional
- **Cost Structure:** Detailed P&L for typical $500k revenue SMB
- **Unit Economics:** Per-technician and per-customer metrics

### 3. MARKET ANALYSIS
- **Market Size & Segmentation:** TAM (US), SAM (SoCal), SOM (acquirable)
- **Growth Drivers:** 3-5 tailwinds with evidence
- **Headwinds:** 3-5 risks with mitigation strategies
- **Regulatory Mandates:** What laws/codes create demand, enforcement trends

### 4. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
- **Market Structure:** Fragmentation metrics, player categories
- **Barriers to Entry:** Licensing, capital, expertise, insurance (scored High/Med/Low)
- **Competitive Intensity:** Porter's Five Forces analysis, overall score X/10
- **Differentiation Factors:** What makes winners win, commoditization risk

### 5. OPERATIONAL DEEP DIVE
- **Licensing Requirements:** Specific licenses, transferability, pathways for acquirer
- **Insurance & Bonding:** Types, coverage amounts, annual costs
- **Equipment & Capital:** Essential equipment list with costs and useful life

### 6. FINANCIAL ANALYSIS
- **Industry Benchmarks:** Margins, metrics, working capital (with sources)
- **BizBuySell Deal Flow Analysis:** Number of listings, asking prices, multiples
- **Base Case Model:** 5-year pro forma with entry/exit assumptions

### 7. STRATEGIC ASSESSMENT
- **Thesis Alignment Scorecard:** Score 1-10 on each of 10 investment criteria (Total: XX/100)

- **AI Resilience Assessment:** Disruption risk score X/10, breakdown by task type
- **Value Creation Opportunities:** 3-5 specific initiatives with investment, impact, payback

### 8. RISK ANALYSIS

- **Top 3 Critical Risks:** Deep dive on highest severity risks
- **Red Flags:** Automatic disqualifiers and major concerns
- **SWOT Analysis:** Comprehensive strengths/weaknesses/opportunities/threats

### 9. ACTIONABLE NEXT STEPS

- **Target Profile:** Specific criteria for ideal acquisition candidate

### 10. APPENDIX

## Special Research Notes

### For Licensing Research
- Identify SPECIFIC license names (e.g., "California C-16 Fire Protection Contractor License" not "contractor license")
- Determine transferability: Does license transfer with business sale or require new application?
- Provide pathways: Can buyer obtain license? How long? Can buyer hire licensed person as "qualifying party"?
- Research cost: Initial licensing fees, annual renewal costs, continuing education requirements
- Check enforcement: Are licenses actually checked/enforced or paper-only?

### For BizBuySell Research
- Search multiple keyword variations (some businesses listed under different categories)
- Document ACTUAL listings (copy business names, listing IDs, for verification)
- Calculate multiples yourself (don't trust listing descriptions)
- Note staleness (listings >6 months old may be unrealistic pricing)
- Look for patterns: Are all listings from one broker? (May indicate thin market)

### For Financial Modeling
- Use REAL data from BizBuySell listings (not hypotheticals)
- Show your work: Document all assumptions clearly in table format
- Test sensitivity: Create data table showing IRR under different scenarios
- Be conservative: Bear case should assume things go somewhat wrong
- Include distributions: Model annual cash distributions during hold period, not just exit

### For Risk Assessment
- Be skeptical: This is investor protection, not marketing document
- Identify common issues: Customer concentration, owner dependency, license transfer problems
- Provide SPECIFIC mitigations (not just "monitor closely" or "manage carefully")
- Rank by severity: Focus deep dive on top 3 highest-impact risks
- Include probability: Risk = Probability Ã— Impact (both matter)

### For Investment Recommendation
- Take clear stance: No hedging with "it depends" - say STRONG BUY / BUY / HOLD / AVOID
- Explain conviction: Why X/10 and not higher/lower? What would increase/decrease conviction?
- Connect to thesis: Explicitly score against Holt Ventures' 8 investment criteria
- Compare to alternatives: Is this better or worse than other business types researched?
- Set decision criteria: Under what conditions should Holt Ventures pursue this (e.g., "Only if customer concentration <25%")?
