## Part 0: Strategic Constraints (The Reality Check)

### Hard Constraints That Limit Your Options

**Constraint 1: Capital Scarcity ($200k total)**
- **Implication:** You get ONE shot. Can't diversify across sectors.
- **Decision:** Must pick sector with HIGHEST confidence of finding right deal in 6 months
- **Anti-pattern:** Researching all 18 business types equally (analysis paralysis, no action)

**Constraint 2: Time Scarcity (10-15 hrs/week, full-time jobs)**
- **Implication:** Can't be "learning" industries for months. Need fast ramp.
- **Decision:** Prioritize sectors where validation is FAST (days not weeks)
- **Anti-pattern:** Deep industry research before confirming ANY deals exist

**Constraint 3: Single-Sector Focus Requirement**
- **Implication:** Once you start owner conversations, you're "declaring" your focus
- **Decision:** Pick sector where you can credibly claim "I'm serious about this space"
- **Anti-pattern:** Talking to owners in 5 different industries (signals tire-kicker)

**Constraint 4: Remote Management Requirement**
- **Implication:** Business must be manageable with weekend check-ins
- **Decision:** Eliminates high-touch, crisis-driven, or craft-dependent businesses
- **Anti-pattern:** Pursuing businesses that "look good on paper" but need daily presence

## Part 1: Research Philosophy (How to Think About This)

### The Micro PE Funnel (What Actually Matters)

Most people research wrong. They try to understand industries deeply before validating deal flow.

**Wrong Sequence:**
1. Deep industry research (market size, trends, competitors) â† Waste of time
2. Build financial models (margins, multiples, returns) â† Hypothetical
3. Talk to owners (oh wait, no one's selling...) â† Too late

**Right Sequence (Micro PE Logic):**
1. **Deal availability** (Are 10+ businesses for sale RIGHT NOW?)
2. **Transition feasibility** (Can we actually operate this post-acquisition?)
3. **Financial viability** (Will this generate acceptable returns?)
4. **Strategic fit** (Does this match our thesis long-term?)

**Why this order?**
- If no deals exist, market size doesn't matter (STOP HERE)
- If we can't transition, great margins don't matter (STOP HERE)
- If returns are bad, macro tailwinds don't matter (STOP HERE)
- Strategic fit only matters if above three pass

### The $200k Investor Mindset

With $200k, you're not a fund. You're an entrepreneur buying one asset.

**Key mindset shifts:**
1. **Speed > Perfection** - You want "good enough to decide" not "institutional quality"
2. **Primary > Secondary** - Talk to sellers directly, don't read reports about industries
3. **Practical > Theoretical** - Focus on "Can I close a deal in 6 months?" not "Is this a good sector?"
4. **Eliminate > Optimize** - Rule out bad options fast, don't optimize between good options

#### Day 4-5 (Thursday-Friday): Quick Financial Sanity Check
**Objective:** Confirm "typical" businesses in remaining types can hit return targets

**Method: Back-of-Envelope Underwriting (3-4 hours)**

**Step 1: Gather Data from BizBuySell Listings**
- Find 3-5 recent listings in this business type
- Extract: Revenue, SDE/EBITDA, Asking Price, # Employees

**Step 2: Calculate Key Metrics**
```
Asking Multiple = Asking Price / SDE
Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue (estimate if not disclosed)
SDE Margin = SDE / Revenue
Revenue per Employee = Revenue / # Employees
```

**Step 3: Build Simple Acquisition Model**
```

- Replace owner salary with manager: -$0 (manager already in place, promote)
- Add owner's benefits back: +$10,000 (health insurance, etc.)
- Adjusted EBITDA: $70,000

Base Case: Hold 5 years, exit at 5x EBITDA
- Exit value: $70k * 5 = $350k
- Return: $350k / $180k = 1.94x MOIC, 14% IRR
```

### Why Owner Conversations Matter More Than Research Reports

**The Problem with Desk Research:**
- Industry reports tell you market size (useless - you're buying ONE business)
- Financial benchmarks tell you averages (useless - every business is unique)
- Macro trends tell you tailwinds (useless if specific businesses have problems)

**What Owner Conversations Reveal:**
- "I spend 80% of my time chasing invoices" â†’ Working capital / collections problem
- "My biggest customer is 60% of revenue" â†’ Customer concentration risk (ELIMINATE)
- "I haven't taken a vacation in 5 years" â†’ Not remote manageable (ELIMINATE)
- "The city inspector knows me by name" â†’ Owner-dependent relationships (RISK)
- "We have 3 certified techs who've been here 10+ years" â†’ Stable, transferable (GOOD)

**The Data You Can't Get from Research:**
- How hard is owner transition REALLY? (Owners will tell you truth if asked right)
- What's the #1 reason customers leave? (Reveals stickiness reality)
- What % of work is truly recurring vs. "repeat customers"? (Big difference)
- How much does owner WANT to sell vs. "testing market"? (Motivation matters)

### Day 9-12 (Thursday-Sunday): Execute Owner Interviews
**Objective:** Complete 15-25 calls, gather intel on each business type

**Interview Structure (15-20 minutes):**

**Opening (1-2 min):**
"Thanks for taking the time. As mentioned, I'm researching [industry] businesses for potential acquisition. I have a technical background and experience with complex regulatory environments [NRC/nuclear], so I'm comfortable with licensing and compliance, but I want to understand the day-to-day reality from someone who's been doing this. I'll keep this to 15-20 minutes."

**Part 1: Operations & Owner Role (5-7 min)**
1. "What does a typical week look like for you? Where do you spend most of your time?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Is owner doing technical work or managing?
2. "How many employees do you have? What do they handle vs. what only you can do?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Owner dependency signals
3. "If you had to be away for 2-3 weeks, what would happen to the business?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Remote manageability test
4. "What skills or knowledge would someone need to replace you?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Speed to competence assessment

**Part 2: Customer & Revenue (4-5 min)**
5. "What does your customer base look like? How many total customers?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Concentration risk
6. "What percentage of revenue is truly recurring? Annual contracts, mandated inspections, etc.?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Verify recurring revenue claim
7. "What's your typical customer retention rate? Why do customers leave when they do?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Stickiness reality check
8. "How do you get new customers? Referrals, marketing, repeat business?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Sales process difficulty

**Part 3: Economics & Operations (3-4 min)**
9. "Without getting into specific numbers, what are the biggest cost drivers in this business?"
   - LISTEN FOR: Labor, equipment, insurance, licensing costs
10. "What's been the hardest part of running this business? What keeps you up at night?"
    - LISTEN FOR: Hidden operational complexity, regulatory risk
11. "If you were starting today with AI/automation available, what would you build differently?"
    - LISTEN FOR: Automation opportunity validation

**Part 4: Transition & Exit (2-3 min)**
12. "Have you seen other businesses in this space sell? How did those transitions go?"
    - LISTEN FOR: Transition difficulty, key person risk
13. "If you were to sell [or for sellers: Why are you selling?], what would be most important to you in a buyer?"
    - LISTEN FOR: Seller motivation, transition expectations
14. "[FOR SELLERS ONLY] What do you think the business needs from a new owner to grow to the next level?"
    - LISTEN FOR: Value creation opportunities

**Closing:**
"This has been incredibly helpful. I really appreciate your time. If I end up focusing on [industry] businesses, would it be okay if I followed up with you down the road? I'm still in early stages, but I want to make sure I'm building relationships with people who really understand this space."

## Part 5: Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake #1: "Let's research all 18 types equally"
**Why it's wrong:** With $200k, you can only pursue ONE sector. Don't pretend you can diversify.
**Right approach:** Eliminate fast, focus deep on 1-2 finalists.

### Mistake #2: "Let's read industry reports first"
**Why it's wrong:** Reports tell you about industries, not specific businesses you can buy TODAY.
**Right approach:** Start with "Are there 10 deals?" then learn about industry.

### Mistake #3: "Let's build detailed financial models"
**Why it's wrong:** Hypothetical models before confirming deal flow = analysis paralysis.
**Right approach:** Rough models (back-of-envelope) for screening, detailed models for specific LOI targets.

### Mistake #4: "Let's only talk to sellers"
**Why it's wrong:** Sellers have bias (want to paint rosy picture).
**Right approach:** Talk to both sellers AND non-sellers for unbiased operational reality.

### Mistake #5: "Let's take 6 months to decide"
**Why it's wrong:** Analysis paralysis, and in 6 months best deals are gone.
**Right approach:** 3 weeks to decision, then 3-6 months to close first deal.
